# Consultation CTA Copy — Research & Cursor Prompt

**Goal:** Replace "schedule your solar consultation" / "review your estimate" with conversion-focused copy that reflects what actually happens next in the solar flow (custom design → proposal → path to installation) and that matches how top solar companies and experts frame this step.

**Constraint (unchanged):** Do not change any horizontal spacing, widths, padding, or alignment in the bottom consultation section of the report page. Only copy and (if needed) vertical spacing may change there.

---

## 1. Research Summary & Sources

### What happens next after a solar estimate (industry flow)

- After a homeowner gets a **quote or savings estimate**, the standard next step is **not** "review your estimate." It is:
  1. **Site survey / design** — installer evaluates roof, shading, system fit.
  2. **Custom design / proposal** — tailored system design, equipment, financing, savings.
  3. **Proposal review** — homeowner compares and decides.
  4. **Contract → permitting → installation.**

Sources: Aurora Solar ("Understanding the solar sales funnel"), Sunrun ("7 steps to going solar"), Solar Surge, TerraSol Solar, Ipsun Solar, Home Team Solar, Energyscape Renewables.

### How major solar companies phrase the next step

- **Sunrun:** "Get a free personalized quote" (initial step); post-quote flow is site survey → design review → permitting → installation.
- **SunPower:** "Get a Quote," "Get Started" — emphasis on "seamless process" and "expert support from consultation to installation."
- **Tesla:** "See My System," "Order Now" on design/checkout.
- **Project Solar:** "Get Your FREE Solar Home Design," "Schedule a call" to get "custom system design, answer questions, and show how we can cut your electric bill by up to 95%."
- **Solarsimple:** "Get Your Custom Design," "See how solar would look on your home."
- **Zeno:** "Request Your Free Solar Proposal & Design."
- **Solar Surge, Conductor Solar, Bloom Renewables, BYOPower:** "Schedule a call," "Book a Call," "Schedule a free solar consultation" — paired with benefits: **custom design**, **proposal**, **next steps**, **cut electric bill**, **honest answers from a licensed expert.**

### Why "review your estimate" underperforms

- **LeadCapture.io (Solar Energy World case study):** Frame the offer as a **qualification or clear outcome**, not a generic request. "Enter your basic information to **see if your home qualifies**" outperforms "Get a quote" because it creates curiosity and positions the prospect as taking a meaningful step.
- **Aurora Solar (CTA best practices):** CTAs should be "short, clear, and powerful" with an **action verb** and a **clear outcome**. Examples that work: "**Build your system for free**," "**Get a free savings estimate**," "**See how much you could save**," "**See solar on your home**." The focus is on what they **get** (design, savings, system on roof), not on "review."
- **LeadCapture.io (9 conversion tweaks):** "Make an offer" in the form — e.g. free consultation, zero-down financing, **custom design** — and reframe data collection as **value exchange**. "Review your estimate" does not clearly communicate that value.
- **Industry pattern:** Post-quote CTAs that convert tie the **call/consultation** to a **tangible next step**: custom system design, proposal, or path to installation. "Review your estimate" sounds passive and does not signal movement toward **buying solar**.

### Urgency (without being sleazy)

- **LeadCapture.io:** Use **credible urgency** (e.g. 30% federal tax credit) rather than fake countdown timers.
- **Solar installers:** "Lock in lower energy costs," "Act now to lock in fixed energy costs," "Claim your 30% Federal Tax Credit before it’s too late."
- For Sunspire’s report page, urgency can be subtle: e.g. "get your design," "take the next step," "lock in your savings" — all imply forward motion toward a decision.

## 2. Recommended Copy (conversion-focused)

Copy is chosen to: (1) reflect the real next step (design/proposal/installation path), (2) use action verbs and clear outcome, (3) avoid passive "review your estimate," (4) stay enterprise-appropriate and non-sleazy.

### Report page — bottom consultation section

**Heading (pick one; recommend A or B):**

- **A.** `Next step: get your custom solar design`  
  (Matches Project Solar, Solarsimple, Generation Solar — "Get your custom design" / "Get Your FREE Solar Home Design.")
- **B.** `Next step: get your proposal & next steps`  
  (Matches Zeno, TerraSol — "Request your free solar proposal & design.")
- **C.** `Next step: take the next step toward solar`  
  (Clear forward motion; a bit longer.)

**Supporting paragraph (pick one; recommend 1):**

- **1.** `You have your estimate. A quick call lets [Company] create your custom system design, answer your questions, and walk you through the path to installation. No obligation.`  
  (Outcome-focused: custom design + path to install.)
- **2.** `Your estimate is ready. The next step is a short call so your installer can design your system, confirm equipment and incentives, and get you to a formal proposal. No obligation.`  
  (More "proposal" language.)
- **3.** `You already have your numbers. Share your details and your installer will reach out to create your custom design and discuss next steps—no obligation.`  
  (Shorter; still design + next steps.)

**Primary CTA button (pick one; recommend 1 or 2):**

- **1.** `Get your custom design`  
  (Short, outcome-focused; used by multiple installers.)
- **2.** `Get your design & next steps`  
  (Slightly longer; clarifies "what happens next.")
- **3.** `Schedule a call to get your design`  
  (Explicit action + outcome; used by Project Solar, Solar Surge.)
- **4.** `Request your proposal`  
  (Direct; matches "Get a proposal" / "Request your free solar proposal & design.")

**Aria-label:** Match the button text (e.g. "Get your custom design" or "Get your design and next steps"). Keep "Download PDF" and "Copy Share Link" as-is.

### Modal (ReportLeadModal)

**Title (pick one; recommend A):**

- **A.** `Get your custom design & next steps`  
  (Aligns with report CTA; clear outcome.)
- **B.** `Schedule a quick call to get your design`  
  (Action + outcome.)
- **C.** `Request your solar proposal`  
  (If you prefer "proposal" over "design.")

**Intro/body (pick one; recommend 1):**

- **1.** `Share your details and [Company Name] will reach out to create your custom system design, answer your questions, and walk you through next steps toward installation. No obligation.`  
  (No "review your estimate"; design + path to installation.)
- **2.** `Share your details and your installer can follow up to create your custom design, confirm system fit and incentives, and discuss your proposal. No obligation.`  
  (Design + proposal language.)

**Consent:** Keep current structure; suggested text:  
`I agree to be contacted by [Company Name] by phone, email, or text regarding my solar project and next steps.`  
(Optional: add Privacy/Terms links as today.)

**Submit button:** Align with report CTA, e.g.  
- `Get your custom design` or  
- `Request my design & next steps`  
(No need for 📅 if the rest of the flow doesn’t use it; keep or drop per brand.)

**Helper text:** `Takes ~30 seconds. No obligation.`  
**Cancel:** `Cancel`

### Sticky CTA bar (report page)

- If it currently says "Book a Free Consultation," consider aligning with the new primary CTA, e.g. **"Get your custom design"** or **"Get your design & next steps"** so the whole report experience is consistent. Update only the `label` prop where `StickyCtaBar` is used on the report page.
